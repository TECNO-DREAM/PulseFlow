üö® Life-Threatening Emergencies

These conditions can cause death within minutes and require immediate action.

Cardiac Arrest

Signs: Unresponsive, no breathing, no pulse.

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. Start CPR: 30 compressions, 2 breaths.
  3. Use AED if available.

Do not delay CPR.

Severe Bleeding (Hemorrhage)

Signs: Spurting blood, soaked clothing.

  1. Apply direct pressure.
  2. Use tourniquet if bleeding continues.
  3. Lay person down.

Do not remove embedded objects.

Choking

Signs: Cannot breathe, speak, or cough.

  1. Give abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  2. For infants: back blows and chest thrusts.
  3. Call emergency services.

Do not perform blind finger sweeps.

Anaphylaxis

Signs: Swelling, breathing difficulty, rash.

  1. Use epinephrine auto-injector immediately.
  2. Lay flat, elevate legs.
  3. Call emergency services.

Do not delay epinephrine.

Stroke

FAST: Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time critical.

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. Keep person seated upright.
  3. Do not give food or drink.

Severe Burns

Signs: Blistering, charred skin, large area burns.

  1. Remove from heat source.
  2. Cool with water for 20 minutes.
  3. Cover loosely and seek emergency care.

Do not apply butter or oils.

ü©∏ Trauma

Physical injuries caused by external force.

Head Injury / Concussion

Signs: Confusion, vomiting, loss of consciousness.

  1. Keep person still.
  2. Monitor breathing and alertness.
  3. Seek medical evaluation.

Do not let them sleep if symptoms worsen.

Spinal Injury

Signs: Neck pain, numbness, paralysis.

  1. Do not move the person.
  2. Stabilize head and neck.
  3. Call emergency services.

Any movement may cause paralysis.

Fractures

Signs: Deformity, swelling, severe pain.

  1. Immobilize the area.
  2. Apply ice.
  3. Seek medical care.

Do not attempt to realign bones.

Burn Injuries

Signs: Redness, blisters, charred skin.

  1. Cool with water.
  2. Cover loosely.
  3. Get medical care if severe.

Do not pop blisters.

Eye Injuries

Signs: Pain, vision loss, bleeding.

  1. Flush chemicals with clean water.
  2. Do not remove embedded objects.
  3. Cover both eyes and seek care.

üåç Environmental Emergencies

Injuries and illnesses caused by environmental exposure.

Heat Exhaustion

Signs: Heavy sweating, weakness, nausea.

  1. Move to shade.
  2. Give cool fluids.
  3. Cool with wet cloths.

Seek care if symptoms worsen.

Hypothermia

Signs: Shivering, slurred speech, confusion.

  1. Move to warm shelter.
  2. Remove wet clothing.
  3. Warm gradually.

Do not use hot baths for severe hypothermia.

Snake Bite

Signs: Fang marks, swelling, pain.

  1. Keep victim still.
  2. Immobilize limb.
  3. Seek emergency care.

Do not cut, suck, or apply tourniquet.

Drowning / Near-Drowning

Signs: Not breathing, coughing water.

  1. Remove from water safely.
  2. Begin rescue breathing/CPR.
  3. Call emergency services.

Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

Signs: Headache, dizziness, confusion.

  1. Move to fresh air immediately.
  2. Call emergency services.
  3. Do not re-enter area.

ü©∫ Medical Conditions

Sudden illnesses not caused by injury.

Asthma Attack

Signs: Wheezing, chest tightness, shortness of breath.

  1. Sit upright.
  2. Use inhaler.
  3. Seek emergency care if no improvement.

Seizure

Signs: Convulsions, loss of consciousness.

  1. Protect head.
  2. Do not restrain.
  3. Place in recovery position after.

Call emergency services if seizure lasts >5 minutes.

Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia)

Signs: Shaking, sweating, confusion.

  1. Give sugary drink or glucose tablets.
  2. Recheck after 15 minutes.
  3. Seek emergency care if unconscious.

Fainting (Syncope)

Signs: Sudden collapse, pale skin.

  1. Lay flat.
  2. Elevate legs.
  3. Monitor breathing.

Panic Attack

Signs: Rapid breathing, fear, chest tightness.

  1. Reassure calmly.
  2. Slow breathing.
  3. Stay with person.

ü©π Minor Injuries & Conditions

Non-life-threatening injuries that can usually be treated at home.

Minor Cuts & Scrapes

Signs: Small bleeding wounds.

  1. Wash with clean water.
  2. Apply antiseptic.
  3. Cover with bandage.

Nosebleed

Signs: Bleeding from nose.

  1. Lean forward.
  2. Pinch nostrils for 10 minutes.
  3. Apply cold compress.

Sprains & Strains

Signs: Pain, swelling, limited movement.

  1. Rest.
  2. Ice.
  3. Compression and elevation.

Fever

Signs: High body temperature.

  1. Give fluids.
  2. Use fever reducers if appropriate.
  3. Seek care if persistent.

Sunburn

Signs: Red, painful skin.

  1. Cool compress.
  2. Aloe vera.
  3. Hydration.
